# Jazz Club Music Handoff

The game treats your recordings like a live band. It can choose compatible
parts, bring instruments in and out, use fills for transitions, change tempo
with tension, and place win/loss accents on the beat.

The audio currently in the game is test material. Your recordings will replace
it.

## First test delivery

Please send one folder containing:

- One main A chord progression recorded with two different chord instruments
- One matching A bass line
- One contrasting B chord progression and its matching bass line
- At least one lead instrument
- One lower-drum part, such as kick and snare
- One upper-drum part, such as hats, ride, brushes, or percussion
- One transition fill
- One small-win accent, one loss accent, and one big-win accent
- One information sheet describing the files

After this first set works in-game, you can add more leads, drum patterns,
tension layers, room textures, fills, and instrument variations.

## Export rules

All looping files in the set must:

- Be uncompressed 44.1 kHz, 24-bit WAV files
- All be mono or all be stereo
- Be exactly 8 or 16 bars in 4/4
- Be recorded at 120 BPM
- Start together on bar one
- Have the same exact length and ending point
- Have no count-in or unequal silence at the beginning

At 120 BPM, 8 bars are 16 seconds and 16 bars are 32 seconds.

Fills and win/loss accents are separate one-time sounds and must not loop.

## File names

Use:

**Environment_Classification_Instrument_PatternNumber.wav**

Examples:

- `JazzClub_Chords_Piano_1.wav`
- `JazzClub_Bass_UprightBass_1.wav`
- `JazzClub_Lead_Trumpet_1.wav`
- `JazzClub_DrumsLow_JazzKit_1.wav`
- `JazzClub_DrumsHigh_BrushKit_1.wav`
- `JazzClub_Fill_BrushKit_1.wav`
- `JazzClub_Stinger_Trumpet_1.wav`

Use these classification words: Chords, Bass, Lead, DrumsLow, DrumsHigh,
Tension, Texture, Fill, and Stinger.

## Musical structure

The first arrangement is:

**A A B A / A A C A**

Each letter is a four-bar phrase.

- A is the main progression.
- B is a different progression with its own matching bass line.
- C uses the A progression and bass line but changes the chord instrument.

For every progression, tell us the key, chords in order, matching bass roots,
and exactly which chord and bass files may play together. If you want another
key, provide a complete chord-and-bass set for that key.

## Information sheet

A simple document or spreadsheet is fine. For each file, list:

- Exact filename
- Instrument and musical part
- A, B, or C section
- Key and chord progression
- Compatible chord and bass files
- Low, medium, or high intensity
- How common or rare the variation should be
- Anything it must not play with
- Preferred reverb, delay, distortion, compression, and loudness

For each fill, also list where it can start and end and whether it needs one,
two, or four bars of warning.

For each accent, list whether it is for a small win, loss, or big win and
whether it should land on the next beat, half-bar, bar, or full phrase.

## What the game handles

The game will keep the files synchronized, prevent incompatible chord and bass
pairings, build and reduce layers, place fills before changes, move the Jazz
Club gradually between about 116 and 124 BPM without changing pitch, and make
win/loss accents part of the song. A big win affects exactly four musical bars.

Before producing the full set, please tell us if you want any fundamental
change to this design. The first test delivery above is enough for us to hear
the complete system working with your music.
