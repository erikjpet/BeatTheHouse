# Beat the House — 0.3.1 Performance & Stability Audit Board

> **HISTORICAL — superseded, kept for reference.** This records the shipped
> 0.3.1 hardening work and its contemporary baselines.

Created: 2026-07-03
Owner: project management
Scope: version 0.3.1 is a hardening release. NO new content, NO new features.
The goal is a robust game that can be played hard without knocking down a
house of cards: find and fix performance slowdowns, duplicate logic, weak
implementations, and unintended performance hits across the whole codebase.

Known debt this board exists to retire (do not rediscover, verify and extend):

- Game surfaces redraw fully in GDScript every frame (slot ~13ms of a 16ms
  budget on a 4070 Ti). R5 added a 16.0ms p95 budget assert that passes, but
  the margin is thin and min-spec hardware has no headroom.
- The per-frame deep-copy regression class has bitten twice (slot bonus
  watchdog at 32.6ms/frame; surface_needs_auto_tick full-table copies). Fixed
  case-by-case via peek paths, but nothing prevents the next one.
- bar_dice_game_suite once took 145s; T7.6/R5 brought resolve times down, but
  suite-time budgets are not enforced anywhere.
- T4.8's 30-run mouse batch recorded 22/30 playable with 8 uninvestigated
  "non-travel investigation failures" under -AllowRunFailures. R8 noted
  "unrelated/pre-existing pinball acceptance and general mouse-playtest
  failures". These were never root-caused.
- 0.3 shipped on a 3-run balance smoke that explicitly superseded the 60-run
  statistical batch. That was a reduced-confidence release decision.

```text
[PREPEND SHARED GUIDELINE]
```

```text
reel motion, meters). Single-pointer hit regions must keep working.
```
