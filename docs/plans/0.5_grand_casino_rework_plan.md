# 0.5 Grand Casino Rework — Design Plan (Owner-Locked)

Status: PLANNED for 0.5. Owner selected and modified every feature on
2026-07-14; the decisions below are locked. This plan supersedes the
*forward-looking* parts of `docs/plans/grand_casino_endgame_design.md`;
that document's canonical ids and save-compatibility rules REMAIN BINDING
(`the_house_calls`, `high_roller_cashout`, `casino_taken_out_back`,
`grand_casino_endgame_state`, and the attention/evidence flag families
must keep their ids). Execution must update that doc where this plan
changes behavior.

## Vision

The Grand Casino stops being one boss room with a hidden state machine
and becomes a living, legible endgame: three connected rooms, a pit boss
who physically walks the floor toward trouble, a rival cast of cheaters
drawing his eye, a chips economy cashed at a teller window, a tiered
Players Card program run by a host who is Rourke's mirror, and a boss
encounter you PLAY — cards against Rourke in the back room — with a
graduated ladder of endings. Two distinct mastery paths: the honest
grinder who climbs the card tiers, and the cheat who can never hold a
card but plays the shadows. Act 1's final wall, and the seam into Act 2.

---

## 1. Spatial rework — three rooms (A1, A3)

- The Grand Casino becomes THREE sub-environments using the walkable
  room pattern (same mechanism as the meta home's rooms):
  1. **Main Floor** — machines and volume play: slots, video poker,
     pull tabs, the casino bar (bar dice at the bar). Contains the Cage
     (section 2), the host desk, entrances to the other rooms.
  2. **High-Limit Room** — table games only: blackjack, baccarat,
     roulette, at boss-tier rule variants (higher minimums, sharper
     dealer/shoe configs — data-driven table configs on the existing
     game modules, NO new game modules). Video poker explicitly does
     NOT appear here (it is a machine; machines live on the main floor).
     Access gated: Silver Players Card OR a steep cash buy-in/bribe
     (the cheater's entrance — see section 6).
  3. **Back Room** — a special BOSS environment. Not freely accessible;
     you are TAKEN there (showdown, section 7) or glimpse others being
     taken there. This is where the Rourke encounter plays out.
- Travel between rooms is in-environment room movement (like home ↔
  rooms), not world-map travel. Heat, attention, and endgame state are
  shared across all three rooms (one casino).
- The Cage is NOT a room: it is an interaction on the Main Floor that
  opens a window (section 2).

## 2. Chips economy + the Cage window (A4, part of B3)

- **Chips**: table games in the Grand Casino (blackjack, baccarat,
  roulette — main floor tables if any, and the high-limit room) pay and
  charge in CHIPS, a Grand-Casino-scoped currency tracked separately
  from bankroll. Machines (slots, video poker, pull tabs) pay cash as
  everywhere else. Chips have no value outside the casino until cashed —
  or until section 8's pawn path.
- **The Cage** is a Main Floor interactable that opens a dedicated modal
  window with a **teller character present** (portrait, dialogue line),
  displaying efficiently in one view:
  - chip balance and cash-out action (chips → bankroll),
  - Players Card status: current tier, progress to next tier, and the
    tier's benefits (section 6),
  - active promotions/comps available to claim,
  - any review state (card review ready, dirty-money hold, banned).
- Cashing out chips is where the clean-route review physically happens:
  the Players Card review (`high_roller_cashout`) becomes a Cage/host
  interaction, not an abstract event.
- Cheat-won chips keep feeding the existing evidence flags; the Cage is
  where a blocked review visibly routes you toward Rourke (existing
  `grand_casino_attention_high_roller_review` behavior, now shown in
  the window instead of implied).

## 3. Rourke as a living agent + rival cheaters (A2, B5)

- **Rourke exists in exactly ONE room at a time** as a living object in
  the environment (rendered, moving between authored spots). His room
  and movement are simulation state, evaluated ONLY at action
  boundaries from a seeded stream — never wall-clock — so runs stay
  deterministic.
- **Movement rule**: he gravitates toward where heat is being GAINED.
  Track recent heat gain per room (decaying accumulator per room,
  updated on action boundaries); Rourke moves toward the hottest room,
  with inertia (he does not teleport every action; propose: moves at
  most one room per N actions, tunable).
- `pit_boss_watch_status` becomes spatial: watch is only active when
  Rourke is in YOUR room; his position/facing is visible on the canvas
  so timing a cheat becomes a read of the floor, not a text field.
- **Rival cheater NPCs**: seeded ambient patrons flagged as cheaters
  (count tunable, e.g. 1-3 per casino day). On action boundaries they
  generate heat events in their room — raising the ROOM's heat pull and
  DISTRACTING Rourke (their heat draws him away from you). They are
  visible characters with tells.
- **Escort events (B5)**: occasionally (seeded event) Rourke catches a
  rival cheater and walks them to the Back Room — a visible scene from
  the Main Floor (the living-world moment: you see what happens to
  cheaters). While escorting, Rourke is OFF the floor entirely — the
  best cheating window in the game, and the game teaches it by showing
  it.
- Rourke also appears RARELY at tier-2 venues pre-Grand (talk-dock
  appearances that feed the existing prior-boss-event modifiers), so
  the back room is the finale of a relationship, not a first meeting.

## 4. Casino memory + daily staff rotation (A5)

- Within a run, the casino remembers across visits (largely existing:
  attention flags, evidence, counters persist — keep and surface it:
  re-entry lines reference what the staff remembers).
- **Across in-run DAYS, the casino soft-resets**: on day rollover,
  seeded staff rotation MAY occur — a new bartender, a new dealer at a
  given table, possibly no change. Rotation effects are real but
  bounded: a new dealer resets that table's local familiarity/tells; a
  new bartender resets bar comp state; rival cheater cast can rotate.
  Rourke and the Host never rotate (they are the constants).
  All rotation draws from seeded streams keyed by day index.

## 5. The Host — keeper of the Cage and the Card (C2)

- A named host character (name TBD at execution; propose "Sterling") is
  Rourke's mirror: Rourke owns the cheat path's pressure, the Host owns
  the clean path's rewards. Both get real dialogue scenes (talk-dock /
  dialogue presentation, same pipeline as existing speakers).
- The Host runs the Cage window and the Players Card program. On the
  clean path the Host is generous: free comps (drinks, chip bonuses,
  a suite rest), and occasionally LOOKS AWAY — a one-time small heat
  forgiveness ("the host smooths it over") when the player slips
  slightly, only while card-eligible and only at low evidence.
- Host standing is tracked (how you treat him, comps accepted/declined,
  card milestones) and feeds card tier progression and review dialogue.

## 6. Players Card tiers — the honest ladder (C1, D2)

- The card becomes a THREE-TIER ladder earned within the run at the
  Grand Casino, replacing the single-checkpoint review:
  - **Bronze**: entry recognition (settled games + small net winnings,
    low heat). Benefits: bar comps, small chip bonus, host dialogue
    opens.
  - **Silver**: sustained clean play (more settled games, higher net
    winnings, heat ceiling held). Benefits: HIGH-LIMIT ROOM ACCESS,
    better comps, one host "look away".
  - **Gold**: the win. Gold review at the Cage = clean victory
    (`demo_victory_route = "high_roller_cashout"` preserved). D2: log
    an Act 2 seam marker on gold victory (`act_two_seam_ready` flag +
    story entry); for 0.5 the run still ends here as the win.
- **Cheat evidence makes the player permanently card-ineligible for the
  run** (existing evidence flags gate all tiers). This forges the two
  paths: honest players climb tiers for access and safety; cheaters
  can never hold a card — they buy/bribe their way into the high-limit
  room, dodge Rourke spatially, and their only ending is the back room
  or leaving. Two hard paths, different skills.
- Tier thresholds are data-driven in the archetype/demo_objective data
  (extend the existing tunable fields table; keep
  `grand_casino_demo_bankroll` objective id).

## 7. The Boss encounter rework (B1 + B2 + B3 + B4)

The showdown keeps event id `the_house_calls` and failure id
`casino_taken_out_back`, and keeps its deterministic seeded-stream rule.
Structure becomes four phases:

### Phase 1 — The walk (B4: ditch one item)

Between "Rourke calls you" and the back room door, the player gets ONE
chance to ditch exactly ONE item. Options are story-driven and
availability-gated:
- **Hand it to a crew member** — only if the player interacted with The
  Crew this run (crew flags exist): item is SAVED (returned after the
  encounter, or waiting in inventory).
- **Trash it** (bin/toilet/planter): item is LOST, small chance of
  being seen (heat sting).
- **Keep everything**: no ditch.
More flavored ditch outcomes may be authored (data-driven list); one
ditch maximum, enforced.

### Phase 2 — Pat-down: contraband punishment tiers (B4)

The back room search evaluates what the player carried in. Tiers:
- **Clean** (no contraband/surveillance gear): no penalty.
- **Minor** (one contraband-class item): item confiscated.
- **Serious** (multiple contraband, or surveillance gear): confiscated
  + a heavy handicap entering the boss game (phase 3).
- **Blatant** (tunable threshold, e.g. 3+ contraband or watched-cheat
  evidence combined with contraband): IMMEDIATE `casino_taken_out_back`
  failure — no game, no talk. Cheating that blatantly forfeits the
  encounter.
The existing item evidence-penalty table converts from silent math into
these visible tiers.

### Phase 3 — The interrogation beats (B1)

3 beats of dialogue confrontation. Each beat, Rourke presents REAL
evidence from this run (the flags already tracked: watched cheats,
attention sources, debts, drink level, prior encounters). The player
counters with choices whose strength comes from run facts (clean-play
history, items held, host standing, crew ties). Each beat's outcome
shifts the terms of phase 4 — the current formula's hidden modifiers
become visible stakes: starting chip stack, how much Rourke cheats, and
what "narrow" means in the ladder below.

### Phase 4 — The hand against Rourke (B2)

The finale is PLAYED, not rolled: a heads-up card duel against Rourke
in the Back Room boss environment — a large, first-class gameplay
element built as a boss variant of an existing table game module
(recommend blackjack heads-up: the count-challenge and skill-cheat
infrastructure already exists; exact ruleset data-driven). Properties:
- Rourke plays across the table (character rendered, dialogue barks).
- The player's skill-cheats are AVAILABLE — but Rourke is the best
  watcher in the game, and HE CHEATS TOO (authored dealer-side edges
  the player can catch/call out, tuned by phase 3 results).
- Stakes and handicaps come from phases 2-3 (stack sizes, forced
  antes, cheat-detection sensitivity).
- Deterministic seeded streams throughout; no wall-clock.

### Outcomes — the graduated ladder (B3)

Based on the duel result and margin:
1. **Walk out clean**: allowed to exit AND cash out at the Cage. Run
   ends as a victory (`pit_boss_showdown` route preserved).
2. **Shown the door**: allowed to LEAVE but NOT cash out — the player
   keeps their CHIPS uncashed and the run ends SUCCESSFULLY (a win with
   an asterisk: score/reward reflects unconverted chips; chips become a
   meta-pawnable item — section 8).
3. **Taken out back**: run fails (`casino_taken_out_back`).
The old single-roll pass/fail is fully replaced; margin state fields
(`grand_casino_showdown_margin` etc.) are repurposed to record the
ladder outcome for the end screen.

## 8. Meta layer — cards and chips as items (C3, C4, B3)

- **Players Card mint (C3)**: winning the clean route mints a UNIQUE
  Players Card collection item into the meta inventory, stamped with
  the run's stats (seed, score, days, tier reached, route). It is
  presented on the final run report screen (integration point with the
  run-end-screen revamp task). Card items are unique-per-win.
- **Card fragility**: Players Cards always sit at CRITICAL durability
  in the collection item schema. Carrying a card into a run and LOSING
  that run destroys the card FOREVER. (The 4-float durability schema
  exists; cards pin the durability float to the critical band and skip
  normal decay/repair.)
- **Prestige runs (C4)**: bringing a card into a run is a prestige run:
  the casino recognizes you — lower starting attention, but higher
  expectations (tighter clean-heat ceiling), and prestige rewards:
  deeper item subcategories / more valuable drops become reachable
  (hooks into the existing collection tier/drop system). v1 scope:
  recognition effects + loss-on-failure + drop-value bonus; deeper
  prestige subcategory trees can grow later.
- **Uncashed chips (B3 outcome 2)**: leaving with chips converts them
  into a "Grand Casino Chips" meta item (stack, value = chip amount)
  that can be SOLD at Sal's Pawn Shop in the meta home for gold —
  Sal fences what the casino wouldn't cash.

## 9. Structure and stakes (D1, D2)

- Both routes stay DEEP and hard: this is the wall before Act 2, and it
  should feel like one. Balance target: neither route is reliably
  beatable without mastering its path (clean: sustained low-heat
  winning across tiers; cheat: spatial Rourke play + surviving the
  duel).
- Act 2 seam (D2): gold-card victory sets `act_two_seam_ready` and logs
  the seam story entry per `docs/plans/act_two_seam.md`; the run still
  ends as the Act 1 win in 0.5.

## Engineering constraints (binding on all slices)

- **Determinism**: Rourke movement, cheater NPC behavior, staff
  rotation, escort events, interrogation evidence selection, and the
  boss duel all run on seeded `RngStream` forks evaluated at action
  boundaries. Never wall-clock, never engine-global RNG. The
  determinism probe must stay self-consistent.
- **Zero-copy per-frame**: living characters (Rourke, cheaters, teller)
  animate presentationally from state snapshots; no per-frame deep
  copies; movement state changes only at action boundaries.
- **Save compatibility**: all new state lives in `narrative_flags` or
  typed fields with explicit to/from-dict coverage; canonical ids from
  the 0.4 design lock are preserved; a mid-showdown save must restore
  to the correct phase.
- **No new game modules**: high-limit variants and the Rourke duel are
  data-configured variants/boss modes of existing modules.
- **UI**: the Cage window and interrogation follow existing
  panel/dialogue idioms; foundation_main gains wiring only (the
  decomposed controller/view-model pattern is mandatory).
- **Suite timeouts**: max(300s, ceil(recorded baseline × 1.5)).
