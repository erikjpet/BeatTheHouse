# 0.5 Cage Rework — The Cashout Cage as a Full Sub-Environment

Status: PLANNED, owner-directed (updated 2026-07-20).

This reworks shipped Grand Casino code. The Cage currently exists as a modal
window on the Main Floor, while Linda and the Players Card systems are split
between that window, automatic run-state progression, and dialogue scenes. The
owner finds that delivery menu-like rather than spatial. This plan replaces the
window with a fourth walkable Grand Casino room containing three physical
interactions: Linda's cashout counter, a casino ATM, and a small gift shop.

This plan intentionally supersedes the statements in
`docs/plans/0.5_grand_casino_rework_plan.md` that say the Cage is not a room or
must be a Main Floor modal. All other Grand Casino and endgame contracts remain
binding, especially the canonical IDs and route meanings in
`docs/plans/grand_casino_endgame_design.md`.

## Locked owner decisions

1. **The Cage is a complete sub-environment.** It is a fourth walkable Grand
   Casino room connected through the existing internal casino-room travel seam.
   It is not a modal window and must feel like a real casino cashout room.
2. **Linda remains the Cage character.** Do not add another teller. Linda keeps
   her warm dialogue and all landed Players Card, comp, promotion, review,
   cashout, and "look away" functionality.
3. **Linda is visually faceless and secretive.** She appears only as a dark,
   living silhouette behind barred teller windows. Her face is never shown.
4. **Linda performs card upgrades.** Bronze, Silver, and Gold are never awarded
   automatically. Once the active tier's threshold is met, the player must
   return to Linda and claim the next tier through the dialogue menu.
5. **Card progression is sequential and non-stacking.** The player cannot make
   progress toward Silver until Bronze has actually been awarded, and cannot
   make progress toward Gold until Silver has actually been awarded. Play above
   the current tier's threshold while an award is pending does not pre-fill the
   following tier.
6. **Debt blocks every card award.** Threshold progress remains visible, but
   Linda cannot award Bronze, Silver, or Gold while casino ATM debt is above
   zero. Clearing debt re-enables the pending award.
7. **The ATM dispenses cash.** Loans add directly to bankroll in $50 increments,
   with no origination fee, up to a $500 borrowing cap. The cash is ordinary run
   bankroll and may be spent anywhere.
8. **Casino debt compounds at 3:00 AM.** At every in-game 3:00 AM boundary, an
   outstanding casino ATM balance increases by 5%. There is no initial fee.
9. **Linda settles debt automatically during cashout.** Chips convert at the
   landed rate and pay casino debt before any cash reaches bankroll. Example at
   the current 1:1 rate: $150 debt plus 200 chips clears the debt and pays $50;
   $150 debt plus 100 chips consumes the chips, pays $100 of debt, and pays no
   cash. Partial debt settlement is valid.
10. **The gift shop takes chips despite debt.** It offers only 3–4
    non-contraband items and remains usable with an outstanding marker.

## Chip exchange and automatic debt settlement

Preserve the landed chip exchange rate (currently data-authored and normally
1:1). Chip purchases continue to debit bankroll and credit Grand Casino chips.

Cashout uses a single atomic result computed from the requested chip amount:

```text
gross_cash_value = chips_redeemed × exchange_rate
debt_payment     = min(gross_cash_value, casino_atm_debt)
cash_payout      = gross_cash_value - debt_payment
new_debt         = casino_atm_debt - debt_payment
new_chips        = old_chips - chips_redeemed
new_bankroll     = old_bankroll + cash_payout
```

- Linda automatically applies `debt_payment`; no second confirmation is needed.
- A cashout that pays debt but yields $0 is a successful action, not an error.
- A cashout that only partially settles debt is allowed and leaves the remaining
  marker visible.
- At debt $0, behavior is identical to the landed cashout path.
- Preserve existing Rourke/showdown and shown-the-door cashout lockouts; the
  debt-first rule does not bypass them.
- The result and story log separately report chips redeemed, debt paid,
  remaining debt, and cash received.
- The mutation is atomic and value-conserving. A failed action changes nothing.

The ATM also offers direct repayment from bankroll. It accepts a player-selected
whole-dollar amount up to `min(bankroll, debt)` plus a clear "Pay in full"
action. Direct repayment cannot spend chips; chip repayment occurs through
Linda's normal cashout action.

## Casino ATM loan and daily interest

### Borrowing

- Default increment: **$50**, data-tunable.
- Default borrowing cap: **$500**, data-tunable.
- Origination fee: **$0**.
- Borrowing adds exactly the chosen amount to bankroll and the same amount to
  casino ATM debt.
- Multiple $50 draws may be taken while the resulting pre-interest balance is
  at or below $500.
- The ATM disables amounts that would exceed the cap and shows the remaining
  borrowing room and the reason.
- Accrued interest may push the balance above $500. The cap limits new credit;
  it does not forgive or clamp accrued debt. No new draw is allowed until a
  valid $50 increment fits under the cap again.
- A loan is possible only through the Cage ATM, but its cash and debt persist
  throughout the run after leaving the Grand Casino.

### 3:00 AM interest

- Default rate: **5% of the current outstanding balance**, data-tunable.
- Trigger: every crossed in-game **3:00 AM** boundary, not midnight and not
  every 24 hours from the moment of borrowing.
- Whole-dollar rule: `new_balance = ceil(old_balance × 1.05)`. This keeps the
  integer-dollar economy deterministic and house-favorable.
- Interest compounds. A $200 balance becomes $210 at the next 3:00 AM.
- If a clock jump crosses more than one 3:00 AM boundary, apply the calculation
  once per crossed boundary in chronological order.
- Debt created after a particular 3:00 AM boundary is not charged for that
  already-passed boundary. Debt created before a boundary is charged when the
  boundary is crossed, even if only one in-game minute has passed.
- A zero balance never accrues interest.
- Store enough accrual state to make save/load idempotent. Loading, opening the
  ATM, changing rooms, or refreshing UI must never charge interest. The next
  charge is determined solely by advancing the authoritative game clock across
  an unprocessed 3:00 AM boundary.
- Each charge creates one concise story/run-journal entry and a player-visible
  notification that states old balance, interest added, and new balance. Batch
  multiple boundaries without losing the exact audit trail.

The casino marker should participate in existing open-debt summaries and the
showdown's open-debt count exactly once, but it has no turn deadline, collector,
forced repayment, collateral, or street-lender conversion. It does not fail a
run by itself.

## Sequential Players Card program

The card is an explicit state machine:

```text
Unranked progress → Bronze ready → Linda awards Bronze
Bronze progress   → Silver ready → Linda awards Silver
Silver progress   → Gold ready   → Linda completes Gold review
```

### Progress rules

- Only the requirements for the next immediate tier are active.
- Track progress in the current tier segment, beginning when the previous tier
  is awarded (or when the run enters the Grand Casino for Unranked → Bronze).
- Once the active tier threshold is reached, progress is capped/frozen in a
  `ready_to_claim` state. Further play before talking to Linda does not count
  toward the next tier.
- Claiming a tier starts a fresh progress segment for the following tier. Do not
  derive Silver or Gold retroactively from lifetime cumulative casino totals.
- Continue retaining total Grand Casino stats for score, reporting, and other
  systems; add explicit tier-segment baselines/counters rather than corrupting
  the totals.
- Existing clean-play, heat, evidence, and eligibility requirements continue to
  apply unless this plan explicitly changes them.

### Linda's card interaction

Linda's dialogue/menu must always show:

- current awarded tier;
- the next tier and each exact requirement;
- current progress for games, net winnings, heat/clean status, and any other
  landed requirement;
- whether the next tier is still in progress, ready to claim, blocked by debt,
  or permanently ineligible because of evidence;
- the exact ATM balance when debt is blocking the award;
- benefits already owned and benefits granted by the next tier.

Bronze and Silver have explicit claim actions and their existing Linda dialogue,
chip bonuses, comps, access, and look-away rewards fire exactly once at claim.
Gold keeps the canonical `high_roller_cashout` route, Gold card mint, dialogue,
victory flags, and Act Two seam.

When debt is above zero:

- qualification progress may reach and remain at `ready_to_claim`;
- Linda refuses every tier award, including Bronze and Silver;
- Gold review cannot begin and `high_roller_cashout` cannot resolve;
- UI points the player to the ATM or debt-first chip cashout;
- paying debt to zero immediately makes the pending claim action available
  without requiring another game.

## Gift shop

- Generate 3–4 offers from a curated allowed pool using a named seeded fork.
- The candidate filter must share the authoritative classification used by the
  showdown pat-down. Exclude both `contraband` and surveillance-class items,
  including any future item added to those classifications.
- Do not maintain a second hand-copied denylist as the source of truth.
- Price every offer in Grand Casino chips. Never fall back to bankroll.
- Buying remains enabled with casino ATM debt and does not automatically repay
  it.
- Use the standard item acquisition, capacity, duplication, effect, story-log,
  and autosave pipeline.
- Stock and prices are generated once for the run/casino visit according to a
  documented data-authored lifecycle, serialize, and do not reroll on panel open,
  UI refresh, room travel, or save/load.
- Purchased offers remain sold out/removed. The display art reflects remaining
  stock without rebuilding every frame.

## State, compatibility, and reporting

Use one authoritative casino ATM balance. Expose the stable state name
`grand_casino_atm_debt` to view models, tests, reporting, and save data even if
the implementation also uses a normalized debt entry internally. Do not let the
integer field and debt array become competing sources of truth.

Persist and normalize at least:

- casino ATM debt and the interest-boundary marker;
- current awarded Players Card tier;
- active-tier progress baseline/counters and ready-to-claim state;
- claimed one-time tier benefits;
- Cage room runtime state;
- Linda's simulation pose/movement state;
- gift-shop offers, prices, and sold state.

Legacy saves default ATM debt to zero, construct valid sequential card progress
from the best available landed card state, and preserve existing awarded tiers
and one-time benefits. A save with an open legacy Cage modal loads into a valid
Grand Casino state with the modal closed and the new Cage room reachable. A
save with Gold review pending keeps that review pending at Linda.

Run report, HUD/objective guidance, journal, accessibility text, and save/load
snapshots must expose meaningful casino-debt and card-status information. Update
all tutorials and coach anchors that currently require `ui.cage_window_open` so
the tutorial walks into the Cage room and uses its physical fixtures.

## Architecture and performance constraints

- Keep `foundation_main.gd` to orchestration/wiring. Put pure arithmetic and
  state transitions in focused run-state/model services and presentation in
  focused view models/components.
- Use typed GDScript, tabs, sparse explanatory comments, and existing component
  patterns.
- All RNG comes from named `RngStream` forks. Opening UI cannot consume RNG.
- Build focused panels on open or state change, not every frame.
- Linda's ambient animation uses cached snapshots and existing liveness/reduced
  motion policies. Do not regress global idle-animation liveness.
- Meta gold remains separate. Casino chips remain casino-scoped. ATM cash is
  real bankroll.
